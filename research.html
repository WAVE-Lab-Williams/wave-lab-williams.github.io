The Richness of Perception

It is not surprising that the human mind is capable of reasoning and thinking about complex properties like physics (e.g. "Will that stack of plates fall?") or navigation ability (e.g. "Can I get out of this maze?") â€” but perhaps such properties are also spontaneously extracted as a part of how we see the world in the first place?

When vision or perception is discussed in textbooks, we find pages and pages about processing "low-level" properties such as color, orientation, or motion. But our research aims to push our understanding of visual processing, exploring how perception may automatically extract even traditionally "higher-level" concepts (e.g. our projects on intuitive physics or unfinishedness) and complex dynamic properties (e.g. our projects on navigational affordances or causal history).
